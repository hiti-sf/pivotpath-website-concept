The Validation Lifecycle, Managed End to End

Define
User Requirements Specification

Captures required functions and regulatory expectations: the baseline every stage verifies against.

Assess
Risk Assessment

Scores each requirement by impact and likelihood, focusing effort where patient safety, data integrity and quality are most exposed.

Qualify
Installation Qualification

Verifies the system is installed, configured and connected as specified before testing.

Qualify
Operational Qualification

Proves the system functions across its operating ranges, including boundary conditions and error handling.

Qualify
Performance Qualification

Confirms reliable performance under real production conditions: fit for routine use.

Release
Release for Use

Reviews evidence and traceability, then authorises the system as validated for use.

Sustain
Periodic Review

Re-evaluates the system at set intervals to confirm it stays validated as processes change.

NoteIQ VMS, explained

The full validation lifecycle on one system — URS, risk assessment, IQ, OQ, PQ, release and periodic review. Deliverables auto-generate, the matrix self-builds, everything version-controlled and audit-trailed.

Every validation type your organisation needs: Computerized System Validation (IT), Equipment Qualification and Utility Qualification (Engineering), Process Validation (Production), Cleaning Validation (QA), Analytical Validation Documents (QC) and Quality Risk Assessments (CFT).

It scans documents for missing steps and inconsistencies before execution, auto-generates IQ, OQ and PQ protocols and reports from templates — authoring in minutes, not days — and summarises them for reviewers.

It divides a protocol across executors to run in parallel, then auto-merges the parts. Executions run via Word, PDF or VMS.

Yes. Immutable audit trails, 21 CFR Part 11 & EU Annex 11 compliant, records instantly accessible. Every deliverable, discrepancy and deviation is tracked to closure — inspection-ready by default.

AI here is assistive and human-reviewed — it drafts, summarises and retrieves, but a qualified person reviews and approves every regulated decision. Outputs are grounded in your approved content, fully audit-trailed, and your data is never used to train foundation models. See our AI Trust Centre for how we use AI responsibly.
