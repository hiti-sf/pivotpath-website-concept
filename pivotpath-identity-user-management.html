The Challenge

Why Identity Management Breaks Down

✎
Manual identity lifecycle

Onboarding, access changes and exits are handled by hand, so each request is slow and inconsistent.

⤢
Access creep

Rights accumulate over time and over-provisioning leaves users with more access than their role needs.

⎋
Offboarding gaps

Accounts are missed at exit, so dormant credentials linger across applications and equipment.

⛉
Audit & compliance risk

Actions taken outside a traceable workflow become findings when the estate is inspected.

⊟
No single source of identity truth

Without one authoritative record, no one can say with certainty which accounts exist for whom.

The Approach

A BOT-Automated Identity Lifecycle

Onboarding

  • Employee information is fetched from the HRMS around five days before joining
  • ID creation is initiated via a service ticket
  • The user ID is created and updated in the identity database

During Employment

  • The user submits a request to add or modify access rights
  • Qualified and approved tickets enter the BOT queue
  • The user ID is provisioned in the application per the request
  • The service ticket is closed

Offboarding

  • At the effective date (midnight) the HRMS is queried for offboarding employees
  • All existing accounts are identified from the identity database
  • Accounts are deactivated across individual applications and equipment
  • The service ticket is closed

The Tools That Drive the Flow

HRMS

The source of employee, joiner and leaver data that triggers each stage of the lifecycle.

Service-Ticket Tool

Captures every request and its approval, so access changes are raised and authorised in one workflow.

Identity Database

Holds a single record of every account, giving one authoritative view of who has access to what.

BOT Queue

Provisions and deactivates accounts automatically once a ticket is qualified and approved.

The Outcome

Standardised, Secure and Traceable

Standardised user management

Consistent onboarding, access change and exit follow one repeatable flow rather than ad-hoc handling.

Security & least-privilege

Access is matched to role and promptly revoked at exit, reducing lingering or excess rights.

Audit-ready traceability

Every action is tied to an approved ticket and recorded against the identity database.

Operational efficiency

Routine identity work is handled by automation, freeing the team from repetitive provisioning tasks.

Identity & User Management, answered

This illustrative solution automates the identity lifecycle end to end — onboarding, access changes during employment and offboarding — so user provisioning and deactivation follow one standardised, traceable flow.

It draws on the HRMS as the source of joiner and leaver data, a service-ticket tool for requests and approvals, an identity database that holds a single record of every account, and a BOT queue that provisions and deactivates accounts automatically.

At the effective date (midnight) the HRMS is queried for offboarding employees; every existing account is identified from the identity database and deactivated across the individual applications and equipment, and the service ticket is closed.

Every access change flows through an approved service ticket and is recorded against the identity database, so each provisioning or deactivation action is traceable to an approved request.
