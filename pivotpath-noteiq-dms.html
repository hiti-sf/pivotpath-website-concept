NoteIQ: document management,
controlled end to end.

NoteIQ controls the whole document lifecycle, from authoring and review to controlled printing and archival, on one audit-ready system.

Storing documents is easy. Keeping them controlled is the hard part.

What NoteIQ changes.

You already have somewhere to keep documents. The harder problem is keeping them controlled: one versioned source of truth, every change and approval on the record, and every copy, share and linked training tied back to it. From authoring to archival, that is what NoteIQ is built to do.

Printing is where most systems lose control.

This is the difference from the document system you already run. In NoteIQ a printout is a controlled, traceable event: the package is built and printed against a trigger, and every copy and reprint is logged. A printed record is as governed as the one on screen.

One controlled library for every document.

NoteIQ, at work.

The same platform, three views: authoring in a built-in editor, moving a document through review and approval, and tracking the tasks that carry it to effective.

Author in a built-in editor

Move it through review and approval

Each process runs its own workflow, from author sign-off through QA approval to effective, with coordinator, due date and every decision on the record.

Track the tasks that carry it

Every review, approval and lifecycle action is a task with a site, department and status, so nothing waits unseen.

NoteIQ document management, explained

The whole document lifecycle on one system: template and authoring, review and approval, controlled release, controlled printing, revision, retirement and archival, all versioned and audit-trailed.

It is what sets NoteIQ apart from a typical document system. Batch and package printing is triggered by an event, such as a production order, and every printed copy and privileged reprint is tied to a full audit log, with executed records scanned back in. Printing becomes a controlled, traceable event rather than an afterthought.
