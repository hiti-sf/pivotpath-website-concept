NoteIQ, at work.

The same system, across the work: a task assigned to the right executor with the record started, and a protocol executed and signed off step by step.

Assigned to the right person, the record started

A task captures its type, entity, document, target date and reviewer up front, then routes to a user, a group or self, with the task's identifiers carried through.

Executed step by step, signed and timestamped

Each step carries its test procedure, expected and actual result, a pass or fail verdict, and the executor's electronic signature with a date and time.

NoteIQ, explained

Validation generates a mountain of manual paperwork: protocols authored by hand, traceability cross-referenced across documents, evidence scattered across systems. NoteIQ runs the whole lifecycle on one system, so deliverables are drafted, the matrix builds itself, and every record stays audit-ready, without moving a qualification decision off a qualified person's desk.

The full validation lifecycle on one system: URS, risk assessment, IQ, OQ, PQ, release and periodic review. Deliverables auto-generate, the matrix self-builds, and everything is version-controlled and audit-trailed.

Every validation type your organisation needs: Computerized System Validation (IT), Equipment Qualification and Utility Qualification (Engineering), Process Validation (Production) and Quality Risk Assessments (CFT).

It scans documents for missing steps and inconsistencies before execution, drafts IQ, OQ and PQ protocols and reports from approved templates, authoring in minutes rather than days, and summarises them for reviewers. A qualified person reviews and approves every deliverable.

It divides a protocol across executors to run in parallel, then auto-merges the parts. Executions run via Word, PDF or NoteIQ.

Yes. Immutable audit trails, 21 CFR Part 11 and EU Annex 11 compliant, records instantly accessible. Every deliverable, discrepancy and deviation is tracked to closure, inspection-ready by default.

AI here is assistive and human-reviewed. It drafts, summarises and retrieves, but a qualified person reviews and approves every regulated decision. Outputs are grounded in your approved content, fully audit-trailed, and your data is never used to train foundation models.
