The Digital Maturity Model

Stage 1
Reactive

The plant responds to events after they happen, on paper-based records and manual checks.

Stage 2
Proactive

Digitised records and connected data let teams act on issues before they disrupt a batch.

Stage 3
Predictive

Golden-batch analytics and predictive maintenance forecast quality and failures ahead of time.

Stage 4
Adaptive

AI-driven insights close the loop so the operation tunes itself towards the golden batch.

Digital manufacturing, your questions answered

Electronic batch and process records (eBMR/eBPR), electronic logs, checklists and registers, and a process historian — deployed through a Paper-on-Glass rollout that digitises the shop floor without disrupting production.

It is integration across the ISA-95 automation stack — from field instruments and control systems (L0–L2) up through manufacturing execution (L3) to enterprise planning (L4) — so data flows end to end rather than sitting in siloed systems.

We model the golden batch by relating Critical Material Attributes (CMA), Critical Process Parameters (CPP) and Critical Control Points (CCP) to the Critical Quality Attributes (CQA) that define a good batch, then use that model for PAT, predictive maintenance and command-room dashboards.

It progresses through four stages — Reactive, Proactive, Predictive and Adaptive — moving a plant from responding to events after the fact towards a self-optimising operation.

Yes — through Pivot Path’s AnomIQ, anomalies are tracked across both enterprise-wide and standalone systems, feeding AI-driven insights and analytics back into manufacturing decisions.
