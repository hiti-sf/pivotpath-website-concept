What We Build and Run

The batch record, executed electronically.

Electronic batch and process records (eBMR/eBPR), eLogs, checklists and registers, and a process historian for time-series data — with review-by-exception across the record.

Every layer of the plant, speaking the same language.

Integration across the ISA-95 automation stack — field, control and execution layers up to the enterprise. Most plants begin with a paper-on-glass rollout, the first step on the digitalisation path, and build towards full integration from there.

A model of what a good batch looks like.

Critical Material Attributes, Critical Process Parameters and Critical Control Points related to the Critical Quality Attributes that define a good run — the golden batch, held run after run.

Problems found while they're still cheap.

Process Analytical Technology on the line and predictive maintenance from historian data — early detection of drift before it fails a batch, and less unplanned downtime across equipment.

One operating picture, from line to leadership.

Command-room dashboards with real-time performance across lines and sites, and AI-driven insights fed back into operating decisions — with anomaly tracking through AnomIQ, our Quality & Compliance platform.

What Holds a Smart Factory Back

When the batch record is still on paper

Records move to glass without stopping production. Release stops waiting on transcription.

When control, execution and enterprise don't talk

We integrate L0–L4 across the ISA-95 stack, so context flows from field instrument to planning system instead of dying between layers.

When equipment is fixed after it fails

Historian data feeds predictive maintenance, so drift is caught before it costs a batch — not explained after it has.

When leaders can't see across lines and sites

Command-room dashboards hold one live operating picture, from a single line to the whole network.
