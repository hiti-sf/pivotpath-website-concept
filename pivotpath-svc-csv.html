Validation FAQ

It is our framework for progressing validation from document-heavy manual practice (CSV 1.0) towards risk-based, largely automated and unmanned execution (CSV 4.0). We assess where a system sits today and move it up the curve, applying a Computer Software Assurance (CSA) approach so effort is focused on the highest-risk functions.

Computer Software Assurance directs testing effort by risk rather than validating every function equally. High-risk, patient- or data-impacting functions get rigorous scripted testing; low-risk functions rely on unscripted or vendor testing. On one programme this focused approach supported CSA across 10,000+ documents while reducing over-testing of low-risk systems.

We cover network qualification, middleware, hardware, analytical instruments and shopfloor equipment across the full URS-to-PQ lifecycle, governed by a validation master plan (CSVMP). We have validated 200+ RPAs, including unmanned protocol execution.

We validate to 21 CFR Part 11 and EU Annex 11 for electronic records and electronic signatures, with documentation traceable from user requirement specification (URS) through installation, operational and performance qualification (IQ/OQ/PQ).

Yes. We deliver across four buckets — managed services, consulting, transformation and automation, and a software platform (NoteIQ). You can engage us to run validation as an outsourced managed service, advise your team, automate the lifecycle, or adopt NoteIQ to manage validation, documents and training digitally.
