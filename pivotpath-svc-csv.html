Validation FAQ

It is our framework for progressing validation from document-heavy manual practice (CSV 1.0) towards risk-based, largely automated and unmanned execution (CSV 4.0). We assess where a system sits today and move it up the curve, applying a Computer Software Assurance (CSA) approach so effort is focused on the highest-risk functions.

Computer Software Assurance directs testing effort by risk and intended use rather than validating every function equally. High-risk, patient- or data-impacting functions receive rigorous scripted testing; low-risk functions rely on unscripted or vendor testing, with the rationale documented and defensible under inspection.

We cover network qualification, middleware, hardware, analytical instruments and shopfloor equipment across the full URS-to-PQ lifecycle, governed by a validation master plan (CSVMP) and extending to automated and unmanned protocol execution.

We validate to 21 CFR Part 11 and EU Annex 11 for electronic records and electronic signatures, with documentation traceable from user requirement specification (URS) through installation, operational and performance qualification (IQ/OQ/PQ).

Yes. We deliver across four buckets — operations, consulting, transformation and automation, and a software platform (NoteIQ). You can engage us to run validation as an outsourced operation, advise your team, automate the lifecycle, or adopt NoteIQ to manage validation, documents and training digitally.
