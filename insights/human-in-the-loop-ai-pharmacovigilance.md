A single missed adverse event can become a regulatory finding, a patient-safety risk, and a
reputational problem all at once. That is why the promise of AI in pharmacovigilance — faster
case intake, cleaner triage, earlier signals — sits alongside a hard question every safety
leader is now asked in audits: who is accountable when the model is wrong?

## The pressure is real, and so is the risk

Case volumes keep climbing while regulatory expectations tighten. Manual case processing does
not scale linearly, and the temptation is to hand the whole pipeline to automation. But
pharmacovigilance is a regulated safety function, not a back-office workflow. An AI that
silently mislabels a serious case, or drops a signal, does not just create rework — it creates
liability. The failure mode is quiet, and quiet failures are the ones that surface late.

## What good looks like

The standard the buyer is measured against is traceability. Under **21 CFR Part 11** and
**EU Annex 11**, regulated records and the decisions behind them must be attributable, legible,
and auditable — every action logged and timestamped. Regulators are increasingly explicit that
AI in a GxP context is *expected* to keep a human accountable for the decision; the model
informs, it does not decide.

That distinction is the whole game. Automation is welcome for the repetitive, high-volume,
low-judgement work. Accountability stays with a named, qualified person for anything that
touches a safety conclusion.

## FAQ

### Can AI process adverse event cases end to end?

It can process the routine, structured parts of the workflow — intake, de-duplication, data
extraction, first-pass triage. Safety conclusions such as causality and seriousness stay with a
qualified reviewer, under change control and full audit trail.

### Is using AI in pharmacovigilance compliant with 21 CFR Part 11?

Yes, when it is deployed correctly. Part 11 and EU Annex 11 require attributable, auditable
records and decisions. Keeping a human accountable for safety determinations, with every action
logged, is what makes an AI-enabled workflow defensible in audit.

### How do you stop AI from missing a signal?

By design: the model triages and surfaces, but signal decisions are made and documented by
people. The value of automation is that it frees expert time to focus on exactly those
judgement calls, rather than on manual data handling.
